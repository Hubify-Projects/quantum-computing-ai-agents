[Pattern] Every Major Tech Wave Follows: Research → API → Agent → Commodity

Confidence: 0.68 Type: Adoption Pattern

Tracking technology adoption curves across AI, cloud, and developer tools reveals a consistent 4-phase pattern:

  1. Research phase: Papers, prototypes, limited access (6-18 months)
  2. API phase: Cloud providers offer managed APIs (6-12 months)
  3. Agent phase: AI agents automate usage of APIs
  4. Commodity phase: Technology becomes infrastructure

Source: Pattern synthesis from historical tech adoption data. Requires empirical validation with quantum computing timeline data.

Research Process Flow

  1. Knowledge Acquisition: Autonomous agents scan academic databases, preprint servers, industry reports, and GitHub repositories for relevant quantum computing and AI agent research.
  2. Confidence Scoring: Each knowledge item assigned confidence score (0-1) based on source credibility, peer review status, and empirical validation.
  3. Pattern Recognition: Cross-reference findings to identify adoption patterns, technological convergence points, and research gaps.
  4. Citation Validation: All claims traced back to primary sources. No existing frameworks presented as novel discoveries without proper attribution.
  5. Synthesis & Documentation: Houston reviews agent-generated findings, provides theoretical direction, and authors final research outputs.

Confidence Threshold & Quality Assurance

All knowledge items are assigned confidence scores (0-1) based on:

  • Source credibility: Peer-reviewed papers (0.8-1.0), preprints (0.5-0.8), industry reports (0.4-0.7), blog posts (0.2-0.5)
  • Empirical validation: Experimental data increases confidence; theoretical claims without validation capped at 0.7
  • Citation traceability: Direct citations to primary sources boost confidence; secondary or missing sources lower it

Open Research Principles

This research mission adheres to full transparency and reproducibility standards:

  • Open Source: All code, data processing scripts, and agent configurations published on GitHub
  • Preprint Sharing: Research findings shared as preprints before formal publication
  • Data Availability: Experimental data and benchmarking results published with DOI
  • AI Collaboration Disclosure: Clear attribution of human authorship (Houston Golden) and AI research assistance (autonomous agents)
  • No False Novelty Claims: Existing frameworks and equations properly cited; only genuinely new contributions claimed as novel
